Robotic Arm

A fully 3D printed, 5 degree of freedom, robotic arm that is programmed (Arduino) and controlled by Android via Bluetooth connection. The application was developed using MIT App Inventor and can be programmed to repeatedly perform a specific sequence of motions. This personal project involved mostly 3D printed components, 5 load bearing servo motors, an HC-05 Bluetooth module and many fasteners.

I was able to quickly acclimatize to my brand-new 3D printer and learn about the importance of bed leveling and double-checking motor fasteners as I experienced a couple failed prints. For the servo motors, I used rubber bands to relieve stress on the larger load bearing servo motors. In the future, I plan to design a mobile, four-wheeled base that can attach to this robotic arm. This added mobility is intended to simulate the autonomous robots used in leading manufacturing factories of the world.
